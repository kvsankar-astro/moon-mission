 BACKGROUND
  SLIM (Smart Lander for Investigating Moon) is a small, light-weight JAXA 
  lunar lander intended to demonstrate high-precision soft landing with 100-m
  accuracy using "vision-based" navigation. 

  SLIM was launched 2023-Sep-06 @ 23:42 UTC from Tanegashima Space Center 
  (Japan) on a H-IIA Launch Vehicle along with the separate X-Ray Imaging and 
  Spectroscopy Mission (XRISM). The telescope separated at 23:56 UTC, the
  SLIM lunar lander shortly thereafter, on September 7 @ 00:29 UTC.

  The lunar landing area will be located in a low-altitude region known as 
  the "Sea of Nectar", having a constant slope of 15 degrees or less, near 
  Shioli Crater at -13.3 deg (S), 25.2 deg. (E).

  After landing, it will conduct science activities until lunar sunset, doing
  composition analysis of rocks thought to be derived from the lunar mantle 
  using a multi-band spectral camera.

  In addition, the SLIM mission also includes a small probe called the Lunar
  Exploration Vehicle (LEV), which separates from the main spacecraft just 
  before landing and performs photo imaging. The mission also incorporates a
  Lunar Retro-reflector Array (LRA) provided by NASA.
