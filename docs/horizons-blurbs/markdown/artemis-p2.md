 OVERVIEW:
  "Time History of Events and Macroscale Interactions during Substorms"
  (THEMIS) was launched 2007-Feb-17 23:01 UTC from Cape Canaveral (USA) using 
  a Delta II 7925-10C launch vehicle.

  Originally part of a constellation of five satellites orbiting the Earth to 
  study the evolution of magnetospheric substorms, elements P1 (THEMIS-B) and 
  P2 (THEMIS-C) were repurposed to study the lunar environment in 2009 and 
  renamed ARTEMIS-P1 and ARTEMIS-P2. ("Acceleration, Reconnection, Turbulence 
  and Electrodynamics of the Moon's Interaction with the Sun")

  ARTEMIS-P2 reached the Earth-Moon L1 Lagrange point on 22 October 2010, and 
  on July 2 of 2011 was moved into an equatorial lunar orbit (achieved 
  2011-July-17) with a periapsis altitude of approximately 100 km, apoapsis 
  of 19000 km, and period of 26 hours. 

 OBJECTIVES:
  The objectives of the repurposed ARTEMIS mission are to study how particles 
  are accelerated at reconnection sites and shocks, how turbulence develops 
  and evolves in Earth's magnetotail and in the solar wind, and the structure, 
  formation, refilling, and downstream evolution of the lunar wake and particle
  acceleration processes within it.
