 MISSION DESCRIPTION
   Lunar CRater Observation and Sensing Satellite. LCROSS was co-launched with
   the Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter (LRO) aboard an Atlas V rocket from Cape 
   Canaveral, Florida. 

   After launch, the LCROSS shepherding spacecraft and the Atlas V's Centaur 
   upper stage rocket executed a fly-by of the moon and entered into an 
   elongated Earth orbit to position LCROSS for impact in Cabeus crater near
   the lunar south pole. 

   On final approach, the shepherding spacecraft and Centaur separated. 
   The Centaur, acting as a heavy impactor, created a 27-m diameter surface 
   crater 5-m deep, ejecting ~350 metric tons into a debris plume rising 
   above the lunar surface (generally staying below 10 km altitude). 
   Impact was ~ 2.5 km/s at an angle of 3.67 +/- 2.3 degrees.

   Following four minutes behind, the shepherding spacecraft flew through the 
   debris plume, collecting and relaying data back to Earth before also 
   impacting the lunar surface, creating a 18x3 m crater and second debris 
   plume containing ~150 metric tons of material. The goal was to assess the 
   ejected material for water-ice, hydrocarbons, or organic chemicals.

   The debris plumes was expected to be visible from Earth- and space-based 
   telescopes 10-to-12 inches and larger, but no ground-base plume sightings 
   were obtained. 

   On November 13, 2009, NASA reported evidence water was detected after 
   the Centaur impact.
