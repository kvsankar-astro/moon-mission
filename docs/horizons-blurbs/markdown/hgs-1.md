 HGS-1 (Asiasat 3, PAS 22)
  Communications satellite launched by Hong Kong (People's Republic of China)
  as "Asiasat 3" for television distribution and telecommunications services
  throughout Asia, the Middle East, and Australasia, with multiple spot beams
  for selected areas. C- and Ku-bands.
 
  Fourth-stage malfunction resulted in failure to reach geosynchronous orbit,
  apparently being designed for a max. payload of 2.4 metric tons which
  Asiasat 3 (and two previously failed launches using the stage) exceeded.
 
  Upon failure, manufacturer Hughes Global Services purchased the satellite
  back from the insurer and renamed it HGS-1. Two successive lunar flybys
  were done to maneuver into geosynchronous orbit by gravity assist, the first
  time such a maneuver was done by a commercial satellite.
 
  In early 1999, the satellite was acquired by PanAmSat, renamed PAS 22, and
  moved to a new location.
 
 Launched: 1997-Dec-24 23:19 UTC (Tyuratam (Baikonur Cosmodrome), Kazakhstan)
