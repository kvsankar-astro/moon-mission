 BACKGROUND:
 The Wilkinson Microwave Anisotropy Probe (WMAP) is a NASA Explorer mission 
 launched 2001-June-30 on a nominal 27-month mission to make fundamental
 measurements of cosmology. There was a 3 month transit to L2, followed by
 24 months of observing for primary mission.

 It has successfully produced the new standard model of cosmology and 
 continues to to collect high quality scientific data in an extended mission.

 Each WMAP observation of the full sky requires a six month period. WMAP 
 orbits L2 in an oval pattern every six months, requiring occasional station
 keeping maneuvers (usually about every three months) to remain in position.

 The WMAP instrument is continuously shaded from the Sun, Earth, and Moon 
 by the spacecraft to allow lower thermal disturbances.
  
 WMAP measures temperature differences between parts of the sky, not
 absolute temperatures. It is a follow-on to the successful COBE mission.

 Operations are scheduled to end Sep 2010.

 MISSION ACCOMPLISHMENTS:
  * Mapped the Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) radiation (the oldest light 
     in the universe) and produced the first fine-resolution (0.2 degree) 
     full-sky map of the microwave sky
  * Definitively determined the age of the universe to be 13.73 billion years 
     old to within 1% (0.12 billion years)
  * Determined curvature of space-time to within 1% of "flat" Euclidean, 
     improving on the precision of previous measurements by more than an 
     order of magnitude
  * Determined ordinary atoms (baryons) make up only 4.6% of the universe 
     (to within 0.1%)
  * Found that dark matter (not made of atoms) composes up to 23.3% of
     universe's mass (to within 1.3%)
  * Determined that dark energy makes up 72.1% of the universe (to within 
     1.5%), causing the expansion rate of the universe to speed up.
  * Mapped the polarization of the microwave radiation over the full sky,
     discovering the universe was reionized earlier than previously believed.
  * Determined statistical properties of the CMB fluctuations appear "random";
     however, there is evidence of possible deviations from simple randomness
     that are being assessed. Significant deviations would be an important 
     signature of new physics in the early universe.
  * Since 2000, the three most highly cited papers in physics and astronomy 
     were WMAP scientific papers. Overview of 5-year results:
         http://map.gsfc.nasa.gov/news/
  * First mission to use Earth-Moon-Sun L2 point as permanent observing site.

 SPACECRAFT PHYSICAL CHARACTERISTICS:
  Total payload mass: 830 kg
  Launch vehicle    : Med-Lite Delta II 7425-10
  Instrument        : Set of passively cooled microwave radiometers
                      (connected to radiator panels with metal straps)
                      1.4 x 1.6 meter primary reflectors for angular res.
                      Differential measurements to one-millionth of degree.
