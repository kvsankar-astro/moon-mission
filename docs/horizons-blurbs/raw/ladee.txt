 SCIENCE MISSION
   Lunar Atmosphere and Dust Environment Explorer (LADEE) is a robotic lunar
   orbiter designed to gather information on the tenuous lunar atmosphere,
   conditions near the surface, and environmental influences on 
   electrostatically levitated lunar dust.
   
   The mission will baseline global density, composition, and time variability
   of the lunar atmosphere before it is perturbed by future human activities. 

   It will also attempt to resolve a mystery from the last time humans were 
   on the Moon, by determining if Apollo astronaut sightings of diffuse 
   emission at 10s of km above the surface were caused by sodium glow or dust.

   The mission is managed and operated by NASA-Ames with launch and technology 
   demonstration support from NASA Goddard and the Deep Space Network (DSN).

 TRAJECTORY
   LADEE launched from the Wallops Flight Facility (Virginia) on a Minotaur V.
   Trans-lunar injection via STAR 37 FM upper stage.

   Nominal mission duration is ~160 days: 30 days to Moon via a series of
   phasing orbits; 30 days for checkout; 100 days of science operations.

   The spacecraft will approach the moon from its leading edge, travel behind 
   the moon out of sight of the Earth, re-emerge, and execute a three-minute 
   Lunar Orbit Insertion maneuver. This will place LADEE in an elliptical 
   retrograde equatorial orbit with an orbital period of ~24 hours.

   A series of maneuvers will then be performed to circularize the orbit at an
   altitude of 156-miles (250-kilometers).  

   The 100-day Science Phase is to occur in an ~ 113 minute orbit that will 
   vary between 20-60 kilometers altitude due to the moon's non-uniform gravity
   field.  During the Science Phase, the moon will rotate more than three times 
   underneath the LADEE orbit. Periselene will occur over the dawn terminator.

   Following the Science Phase, a decommissioning period is planned, during 
   which the altitude will be reduced until the spacecraft impacts the lunar 
   surface.

 SPACECRAFT:
   Mass : 383 kg
   Power: 295 watts
   Comm : S-band with 10 Kbps science data rate

 LADEE SCIENCE PAYLOAD (50 kg total): 
   UVS : Ultraviolet and Visible Light Spectrometer (atmospheric composition)
   NMS : Neutral Mass Spectrometer (atmospheric variations)
   LDEX: Lunar Dust Experiment (collect and analyze dust samples)

   Lunar Laser Communications Technology Demonstration (32.8 kg, 136.5 watts) 
     Designed to demonstrate a sending and receiving data from missions using 
     lasers instead of radio signals. The laser link from Earth to lunar orbit 
     will have a down-link data rate of 622 Mb/s, compared to current radio 
     methods (about 100 Mb/s).
